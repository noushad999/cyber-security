# Common Cybersecurity Attacks and Their Effectiveness

This document covers various **cybersecurity attacks**, how they operate, and their effectiveness. Understanding these attacks helps in building better defense mechanisms and preparing countermeasures.

---

## 1. Phishing Attacks

**Phishing** is the use of digital communication to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information or deploying malicious software.

### Types of Phishing:
<details>
<summary><strong>Business Email Compromise (BEC)</strong></summary>
<p>
A threat actor sends an email that seems to be from a known or trusted source, attempting to obtain sensitive information for financial gain.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Spear Phishing</strong></summary>
<p>
A highly targeted attack, spear phishing focuses on specific individuals or groups, making the email appear to come from a trusted entity.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Whaling</strong></summary>
<p>
A form of spear phishing, **whaling** targets high-level executives, aiming to access critical information or perform financial fraud.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Vishing</strong></summary>
<p>
**Vishing** (voice phishing) uses phone calls to impersonate legitimate sources to gather sensitive information.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Smishing</strong></summary>
<p>
A form of phishing via SMS text messages, where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive data.
</p>
</details>

---

## 2. Malware Attacks

**Malware** refers to software specifically designed to damage or disrupt devices or networks. Malware can take many forms, often with the goal of financial gain or intelligence collection.

### Types of Malware:
<details>
<summary><strong>Viruses</strong></summary>
<p>
Malicious code that requires user interaction (e.g., opening an infected file or attachment) to activate and spread, damaging data and software.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Worms</strong></summary>
<p>
Unlike viruses, **worms** can replicate and spread independently, affecting other systems without requiring user action.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Ransomware</strong></summary>
<p>
A type of malware that encrypts a victim’s data and demands payment for the decryption key.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Spyware</strong></summary>
<p>
Spyware gathers and transmits data from infected devices, often without the user’s consent, to a threat actor.
</p>
</details>
